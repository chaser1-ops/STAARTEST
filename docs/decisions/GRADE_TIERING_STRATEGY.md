# Grade Tiering Strategy — 2026-04-04

## Problem
Adding K-2 creates grades with less content depth than Grades 3-6. If presented identically, the site would feel uneven.

## Solution: Intentional Quality Tiers
Rather than pretending all grades are equally mature, the site uses clear visual tier indicators.

### Tier Labels
- **Starter HQ** (cyan badge): Kindergarten, Grade 1, Grade 2
- **Full HQ** (lime badge): Grades 3, 4, 5, 6

### Visual Treatment
- Landing page separates tiers with section headers: "Starter Zones — Building Blocks" and "Full Study Labs — STAAR Ready"
- Each grade card shows its tier badge prominently
- Starter HQ pages use simpler language and fewer sections
- Full HQ pages have the complete feature set

### Content Depth by Tier
| Grade | Questions | Subjects | PDF Pack | Boss Battle |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------|-------------|
| K | 12 | Reading, Math | No | Yes (simplified) |
| 1 | 12 | Reading, Math | No | Yes (simplified) |
| 2 | 14 | Reading, Math | No | Yes (simplified) |
| 3 | 32 | Reading, Math | Yes | Yes |
| 4 | 40 | Reading, Math | Yes | Yes |
| 5 | 48 | Reading, Math, Science | Yes | Yes |
| 6 | 40 | Reading, Math | Yes | Yes |

### Why This Works
- No grade link feels abandoned or sloppy
- Users understand the maturity difference without losing trust
- Starter grades still deliver real value (practice, missions, badges, progress)
- Sets expectation for future content growth
