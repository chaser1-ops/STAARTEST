# K-6 Platform Direction — 2026-04-04

## Decision
Expand STAAR Power Portal from a Grades 3-6 study site into a coherent K-6 learning platform.

## Rationale
- A K-6 scope better serves families with multiple children
- Early grade foundations (K-2) build the skills that STAAR tests in Grades 3-6
- Broader scope strengthens brand identity as a platform, not just a test prep page
- Positions the product for future Rise Intellect / RiseIQ platform migration

## Implementation
- **Kindergarten, Grade 1, Grade 2**: "Starter HQ" pages with age-appropriate content, lighter question banks (10-14 questions each), and study tips
- **Grades 3-6**: "Full HQ" pages with deep question banks (32-48 questions), STAAR-aligned cheat sheets, memory tricks, and full feature set
- **Landing page**: Clearly signals K-6 with tiered grade cards separated into "Starter Zones" and "Full Study Labs"

## Grade Tiering Model
| Tier | Grades | Content Depth | Features |
|------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Starter HQ | K, 1, 2 | 10-14 questions, basic cheat sheets | Practice, missions, badges, dashboard |
| Full HQ | 3, 4, 5, 6 | 32-48 questions, STAAR cheat sheets, memory tricks | All features + Boss Battle, Science (G5), PDF power packs |

## K-2 Content Philosophy
- Age-appropriate and playful, not dumbed down
- Focus on building-block skills: letters, numbers, shapes, patterns, basic operations
- Shorter sessions, more encouragement
- No STAAR pressure framing — these grades don't take STAAR

## School Context
- Harmony Science Academy (Garland, TX) remains the primary design lens
- K-2 content is broadly applicable — not school-specific
- Future school-branded editions can include or exclude K-2 tiers as needed
