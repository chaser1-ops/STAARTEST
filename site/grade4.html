Grade 4 skills and strategies.

Build on what you learned in Grade 3 with stronger reading analysis and multi-step math.

Grade 4 reading focus

  • Inference: Use clues + what you already know.
  • Figurative language: Similes, metaphors, personification.
  • Author's purpose: Persuade, inform, entertain — look for evidence.
  • Text structure: How is the information organized?

Answering strategy

  • Read the question before the passage.
  • Underline key words in the question.
  • Go back to find proof — don't rely on memory.
  • Eliminate answers that don't have text support.

Memory Trick: P.A.W.

Passage • Answer choices • Why does the text prove it?

Writing tips

  • Answer the question directly first.
  • Support with two details from the text.
  • Use transition words (also, because, for example).

Grade 4 math zones

  • Multi-digit multiplication and division
  • Equivalent fractions and comparing fractions
  • Geometry: angles, lines, symmetry
  • Multi-step word problems

Key strategies

  • Break it down. Multi-step means do one part at a time.
  • Draw it. Sketch shapes, fraction bars, number lines.
  • Estimate. Does my answer make sense?
  • Label units. Always include the unit in your answer.

Memory Trick: R.U.N.

Read the problem • Underline the target • Number sentence

Check yourself

  • Did I answer every part of the question?
  • Did I use the right operation for each step?
  • Does my answer make sense in context?

Night before

  • Pack supplies.
  • No late cramming.
  • Solid sleep.

Morning of

  • Eat protein + drink water.
  • Say: "One question at a time."
  • Arrive calm.

During the test

  • Mark hard questions and move on.
  • Use your process.
  • Come back with fresh eyes.

Mindset

"I'm ready for this. I've been practicing the right way."
