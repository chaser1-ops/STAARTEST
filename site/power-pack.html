Reading Boost

Works for Grades 3–6. Adjust the complexity of the passage.

1. Read the question first.
2. Find proof in the passage.
3. Cross out weak answers.
4. Pick the answer with the best proof.
Main Idea: What is it mostly about?
Detail: What proves it?
Word Meaning: What clues are nearby?
Author's Purpose: Inform, persuade, or entertain?

Math Boost

Keep this near the workspace. Works for all elementary grades.

R.U.N.
Read the problem.
Underline the target.
Number sentence.
Ask yourself:
• What operation do I need?
• Does my answer make sense?
• Did I answer the whole question?
• Did I include the right units?

Grade-Specific Memory Tricks

Grades 3–4: P.A.W.

Passage • Answer choices • Why does the text prove it?

For reading comprehension questions. Slows you down just enough to find proof.

Grade 5: S.T.A.R.

Scan the question • Text evidence • Answer • Recheck

For more complex reading passages with deeper analysis.

Grade 6: C.L.A.I.M.

Claim • Link to text • Analyze • Infer • Main point

For argumentative and analytical reading at the middle school level.

All Grades Math: R.U.N.

Read the problem • Underline the target • Number sentence

Turn the story into math. Then solve it.
