Grade 5 skills and strategies.

More complex texts, multi-step problems, and deeper analysis. You've got this.

Grade 5 reading focus

  • Theme: What lesson does the story teach? Look at how characters change.
  • Point of view: First person (I/we) vs. third person (he/she/they).
  • Text structure: Problem-solution, cause-effect, compare-contrast, sequence.
  • Vocabulary: Use prefixes, suffixes, and context clues together.

Advanced strategies

  • Annotate as you read — underline, star, question mark.
  • Summarize each paragraph in the margin in your own words.
  • Compare what two passages say about the same topic.
  • Always go back to the text for proof before answering.

Memory Trick: S.T.A.R.

Scan the question • Text evidence • Answer • Recheck

Writing response tips

  • Restate the question in your answer.
  • Use two specific text details.
  • Explain how the details support your answer.

Grade 5 math zones

  • Decimals: adding, subtracting, multiplying, dividing
  • Fractions: adding/subtracting unlike denominators
  • Volume of rectangular prisms
  • Order of operations and expressions
  • Coordinate planes (x, y)

Key strategies

  • Line up decimals when adding or subtracting.
  • Find common denominators before combining fractions.
  • PEMDAS: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiply/Divide, Add/Subtract.
  • Draw models for fractions and area problems.

Memory Trick: R.U.N.

Read the problem • Underline the target • Number sentence

Check yourself

  • Did I follow the order of operations?
  • Are my decimal points aligned?
  • Does my answer make sense in the real world?

Matter and Energy

  • States of matter: Solid, liquid, gas. Particles move faster as temperature rises.
  • Mixtures vs solutions: Mixtures can be separated; solutions are evenly mixed.
  • Physical vs chemical changes: Physical = reversible (ice melting). Chemical = new substance (burning).

Ecosystems

  • Food chains: Producer → Consumer → Decomposer.
  • Adaptations: Traits that help organisms survive in their environment.
  • Inherited vs learned: Eye color is inherited. Riding a bike is learned.

Earth and Space

  • Water cycle: Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation → Collection.
  • Weather vs climate: Weather is daily; climate is the long-term pattern.
  • Earth's rotation: Causes day and night (24-hour cycle).

Force and Motion

  • Force: A push or pull that changes an object's motion.
  • Gravity: Pulls everything toward Earth's center.
  • Friction: Slows objects down. More friction = harder to slide.
  • Speed: Distance divided by time.

Night before

  • Organize supplies.
  • Light review only.
  • Early bedtime.

Morning of

  • Good breakfast.
  • Positive self-talk.
  • Deep breaths before starting.

During the test

  • Skip and return to hard questions.
  • Show all work on math.
  • Re-read questions carefully.
